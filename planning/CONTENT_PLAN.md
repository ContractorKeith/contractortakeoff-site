# CONTENT_PLAN

## contractor-bid: YouTube + X Content Plan

Version 1.0 | 2026-07-01

Channel jobs: YouTube (@contractorkeith) sells with demos and build-in-public. X (@ContractorKeith) keeps the contrarian voice and points at the demos. LinkedIn is a copy machine for Erin. Every piece ends with exactly one CTA.

Production rules: screen-first, real (sanitized) bid documents, terminal segments recorded with VHS or plain screen capture, face cam optional in a corner, hook in the first 10 seconds, no intro music, no "hey guys welcome back." Thumbnails: plain bold text + one real screenshot. No arrows, no shocked faces.

## 1. Welcome video (60 to 90 seconds, publish Day 3, also the channel trailer)

Script (read it nearly verbatim; it's written for your voice):

> I've been in construction for 37 years. Master plumber, journeyman electrician, built and sold a homebuilding company, and right now I estimate commercial fence for a living.
>
> Which means every week I get bid invites with four hundred pages of plans, a spec book, three addenda, and a due date. And before I can price a single foot of fence, I burn an afternoon just figuring out which pages are even mine.
>
> So I built a tool that does that part. It's called contractor-bid. It takes the bid package, finds my scope pages, pulls the specs, builds the takeoff workbook, flags the traps, and packages the supplier sendoff. On my machine. Nothing uploaded anywhere. Every number traces back to a source page.
>
> This channel is me using it on real bids, improving it in public, and showing exactly how AI fits into estimating without letting it invent your numbers.
>
> The tool's free and open source. Link below. If you bid work for a living, stick around.

CTA: repo link + contractortakeoff.ai in description. Pin a comment with the install one-liner.
